Seminar Presentation: RODE and Role-Based Multi-Agent Learning

Multi-Agent Reinforcement LearningRole DecompositionResearch Presentation

Executive Summary

This seminar presentation analyzed and explained RODE: Learning Roles to Decompose Multi-Agent Tasks, an existing research paper presented at ICLR 2021. The presentation focused on how the paper decomposes Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning tasks into role-specific action spaces and uses a bi-level hierarchy to improve coordination; it was not my own paper or original implementation.

Tech Stack / Tools

  • Multi-Agent Reinforcement Learning concepts
  • Role decomposition and k-means clustering
  • QMIX-style mixing networks
  • StarCraft Multi-Agent Challenge (SMAC) evaluation environment
  • Technical presentation and seminar handout

Current Status

Completed seminar presentation. My contribution was reviewing the published work, synthesizing its technical approach and reported results, and communicating them through presentation slides and a handout.

Key Achievements & Functionality

  • Explained the motivation for reducing large joint action spaces through learned agent roles.
  • Presented the paper’s action-effect embeddings and bi-level role-selection and execution hierarchy.
  • Summarized the authors’ experimental setup and reported performance across SMAC maps.
  • Discussed the paper’s reported zero-shot transfer behavior and implications for multi-agent coordination.
  • Prepared presentation material and a concise seminar handout for the technical discussion.
